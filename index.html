Common Routes

Bosques

If you are starting to climb and want to practice on short — but very steep — hills, Bosques De las Lomas is the right place for you. Its residential nature means sometimes you won't find any traffic at all, so you can train with no inconvenience.

El Ajusco

Starting near famous amusement park Six Flags, El Ajusco features Mexico City's longest climb, with almost 35km long and around 1200m of gained altitude. Filled with clean air and delicious tradicional restaurants, this route offers one of the best scenery in the city.

Los Dinamos

Great for a hike and for your bike, Los Dinamos provides a greater culinary and natural variety than El Ajusco, at the cost of horrible pavement and steeper climbs. Feeling adventurous?

La Venta

One of Mexico City's main entrances, La Venta has around ~800m of gained altitude, plus one of the best mantained highways. Be sure to test your brakes before, since you can easily reach 70kph on your way back.

La Loma

A spot just outside Mexico City can be great for relaxation, specially after climbing 20km on the the most windy part of the city. A couple of beatiful pueblos awaits on the other side, should you want to continue, or you could return to Xochimilco, land of the best pulque in Mexico and the world.

AHR

Love the flat lands? Then Autódromo Hnos. Rodríguez is meant for you. With a ~4km lap and built for speed, its pavement will unleash your wildest sprinter inside.
